PLAYERS: 3+

SUMMARY: Order in the Court is a story-building improv game where players assume the roles of various characters in a court case and argue to reveal the truth using witnesses, evidence, and wit. The rules are fairly loose, please feel free to play however your group would like to!

GAME START: The Judge starts the game once the Plaintiff, the Defendant, and all the Witnesses have joined. The Judge then decides how to begin the game. Typically, this is done by the Judge introducing all the players and their roles, along with the issue, then allowing opening statements from the Plaintiff and the Defendant.

GAME END: Once all the evicence has been revealed, the Judge ends the game by making a verdict. Usually, the Plaintiff and Defendant make closing statements before the Judge makes a verdict. The Judge also might consult the Jury players.

  1. EVIDENCE: Each side (Plaintiff & Defendant) has some amount of Evidence. They can reveal this Evidence to prove their case - if they can find ways to connect their Evidence to their argument.
  2. WITNESSES: If you have more than 3 players, you may have Witnesses. You may ask the Witnesses questions to help make your argument.
  3. WIT: When the floor is yours to speak, you can make your case however you want! It is best to stick to Evidence and Witnesses, but any other points are also worth making.
